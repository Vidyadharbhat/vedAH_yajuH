०१

मूलम् - Weber

स᳘र्वे ह वै᳘ देवाः᳟॥
अ᳘ग्रे सदृ᳘शा आसुः स᳘र्वे पु᳘ण्यास्ते᳘षाᳫं स᳘र्वेषाᳫं सदृशानाᳫं स᳘र्वेषाम् पु᳘ण्यानां त्र᳘यो ऽकामयन्तातिष्ठा᳘वानः स्यामे᳘त्यग्निरि᳘न्द्रः॥

Eggeling
  1. Now, at first the gods were all alike, all good. Of them, being all alike, all good, three desired, ‘May we be superior 1!’–Agni, Indra, and Sūrya.

०२

मूलम् - Weber

ते᳘ ऽर्चन्तः श्रा᳘म्यन्तश्चेरुः॥
त᳘ एता᳘नतिग्रा᳘ह्यान्ददृशुस्तान᳘त्यगृह्णत तद्य᳘देनानत्य᳘गृह्णत त᳘स्मादतिग्रा᳘ह्या ना᳘मॗ ते ऽतिष्ठा᳘वानो ऽभवन्य᳘थैत᳘ एत᳘दतिॗष्ठेवातिॗष्ठेव ह वै᳘ भवति य᳘स्यैवं᳘ विदु᳘ष एतान्ग्र᳘हान्गृह्ण᳘न्ति॥

Eggeling
  1. They went on praising and toiling. They saw those Atigrāhyas 3, and drew (grah) them for themselves

over and above (ati): hence the name Atigrāhyas. They became superior, even as they are now superior 4 and verily he becomes superior for whomsoever, knowing this, they draw those cups of Soma.

०३

मूलम् - Weber

नो ह वा᳘ इदम᳘ग्रे ऽग्नौ व᳘र्च आस॥
य᳘दिद᳘मस्मिॗन्व᳘र्चः सो ऽकामयतेदम् म᳘यि व᳘र्चः स्यादि᳘ति स᳘ एतं ग्र᳘हमपश्यत्त᳘मगृह्णीत त᳘तो ऽस्मिन्नेतद्व᳘र्च आस॥

Eggeling
  1. And at first there was not in Agni that lustre which is now in him. He desired, ‘May that lustre be in me!’ He saw this graha, and drew it for himself, and henceforth that lustre was in him.

०४

मूलम् - Weber

नो ह वा᳘ इदम᳘ग्र इ᳘न्द्र ओ᳘ज आस॥
य᳘दिद᳘मस्मिन्नोॗजः सो ऽकामयतेदम् मय्यो᳘जः स्यादि᳘ति स᳘ एतं ग्र᳘हमपश्यत्त᳘मगृह्णीत त᳘तो ऽस्मिन्नेतदो᳘ज आस॥

Eggeling
  1. And at first there was not in Indra that power which is now in him. He desired, ‘May that power be in me!’ He saw this graha and drew it for himself, and henceforth that power was in him.

०५

मूलम् - Weber

नो ह वा᳘ इदम᳘ग्रे सू᳘र्ये भ्रा᳘ज आस॥
य᳘दिद᳘मस्मिन्भ्राॗजः सो ऽकामयतेदम् म᳘यि भ्रा᳘जः स्यादि᳘ति स᳘ एतं ग्र᳘हमपश्यत्त᳘मगृह्णीत त᳘तो ऽस्मिन्नेतद्भ्रा᳘ज आसैता᳘नि ह वै ते᳘जांस्येता᳘नि वीॗर्याण्यात्म᳘न्धत्ते य᳘स्यैवं᳘ विदु᳘ष एतान्ग्र᳘हान् गृह्णन्ति॥

Eggeling
  1. And at first there was not in Sūrya that splendour which is now in him. He desired, ‘May that splendour be in me!’ He saw this graha and drew it for himself, and henceforth that splendour was in him. And verily for whomsoever, knowing this, they draw those cups of Soma, he takes unto himself those same fires (energies), those same powers.

०६

मूलम् - Weber

तान्वै᳘ प्रातःसवने᳘ गृह्णीयात्॥
आग्रयणं᳘ गृहीॗत्वात्मा वा᳘ आग्रयणो᳘ बहु वा᳘ इद᳘मात्म᳘न ए᳘कैकम᳘तिरिक्तं क्लोमहृदयं᳘ त्वद्य᳘त्त्वत् 5 ॥

Eggeling
  1. Let him draw them at the morning pressing, after drawing the Āgrayaṇa; for the Āgrayaṇa is the self (body), and many parts of this self are one each (and thus) over and above (the others), such as the lung 6 and heart, and others.

०७

मूलम् - Weber

मा᳘ध्यन्दिने वैनान्त्स᳘वने गृह्णीयात्॥
उक्थ्यं᳘ गृहीॗत्वोपाकरिष्य᳘न्वा पूतभृ᳘तो ऽय᳘ᳫं᳘ ह वा᳘ अस्यैषो᳘ ऽनिरुक्त आत्मा य᳘दुॗक्थ्यः᳘ सो एषा᳘ मीमाॗᳫंॗसैव᳘ प्रातःसवन᳘ एॗवैनान्गृह्णीयादाग्रयणं᳘ गृहीत्वा॥

Eggeling
  1. Or he might draw them from the Pūtabhr̥t, at the midday pressing, after drawing the Ukthya, or when about to bespeak (the chant), for the Ukthya indeed is the same as that undefined self of his. But this is mere speculation: let him rather draw them at the morning pressing, after drawing the Āgrayaṇa.

०८

मूलम् - Weber

ते᳘ माहेन्द्र᳘स्यैवा᳘नु हो᳘मᳫं हूयन्ते॥
एष वा इ᳘न्द्रस्य नि᳘ष्केवल्यो ग्र᳘हो य᳘न्माहेन्द्रो᳘ ऽप्यस्यैतन्नि᳘ष्केवल्यमेव᳘ स्तोत्रं नि᳘ष्केवल्यं शस्त्रमि᳘न्द्रो वै य᳘जमानो य᳘जमानस्य वा᳘ एते का᳘माय गृह्यन्ते त᳘स्मान्माहेन्द्र्र᳘स्यैवा᳘नु हो᳘मᳫं हूयन्ते॥

Eggeling
  1. They are offered after the offering of the Māhendra graha; for that, the Māhendra, is Indra’s special cup; and so are the (Nishkevalya) stotra and śastra specially his. But the sacrificer is Indra; and for the sacrificer’s benefit (these cups) are drawn: therefore they are offered after the offering of the Māhendra graha.

०९

मूलम् - Weber

अथा᳘तो गृह्णा᳘त्येव᳟॥
अग्ने प᳘वस्व स्व᳘पा अस्मे व᳘र्चः सुवी᳘र्यम् द᳘धद्रयिम् म᳘यि पो᳘षम् उपयाम᳘गृहीतो ऽस्यग्न᳘ये त्वा व᳘र्चस एष᳘ ते यो᳘निरग्न᳘ये त्वा व᳘र्चसे॥

Eggeling
  1. He thus draws them therefrom [the first with

Vāj. S. VIII, 38; Rig-veda IX, 66, 21] 7, ‘O Agni, skilful in works, become thou pure, bestowing upon us lustre and manly vigour, and upon me health and wealth!–Thou art taken with a support: thee to Agni, for lustre!–This is thy womb: thee to Agni, for lustre!’

१०

मूलम् - Weber

उत्ति᳘ष्ठन्नो᳘जसा॥
सह᳘ पीत्वी शि᳘प्रे अवेपयः सो᳘ममिन्द्र चमू᳘सुतम् उपयाम᳘गृहीतो ऽसी᳘न्द्राय त्वौ᳘जस एष᳘ ते यो᳘निरि᳘न्द्राय त्वौ᳘जसे॥

Eggeling
  1. [The second with Vāj. S. VIII, 39; Rig-veda VIII, 76, 10], ‘Uprising by thy power didst thou move thy jaws, O Indra, drinking the cup-drawn juice!–Thou art taken with a support: thee to Indra for power!–This is thy womb: thee to Indra for power!’

११

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘दृश्रमस्य केत᳘वः॥
वि᳘ रश्म᳘यो ज᳘नां अ᳘नु भ्रा᳘जन्तो अग्न᳘यो यथा उपयाम᳘गृहीतो ऽसि सू᳘र्याय त्वा भ्राजा᳘यैष᳘ ते यो᳘निः सू᳘र्याय त्वा भ्राजाये᳘ति॥

Eggeling
  1. [The third with Vāj. S. VIII, 40; Rig-veda I, 50, 3], ‘His beacons have appeared, his beams, wide and far over the people, shining splendidly like fires!–Thou art taken with a support: thee to Sūrya for splendour!–This is thy womb: thee to Sūrya for splendour!’

१२

मूलम् - Weber

ते᳘षाम् भ᳘क्षः॥
अ᳘ग्ने वर्चस्विन्व᳘र्चस्वांस्त्वं᳘ देवेष्व᳘सि व᳘र्चस्वानह᳘म् मनुॗष्येषु भूयासमि᳘न्द्रौजिष्ठौ᳘जिष्ठस्त्वं᳘ देवेष्वस्यो᳘जिष्ठो ऽह᳘म् मनुॗष्येषु भूयासᳫं सू᳘र्य भ्राजिष्ठ भ्रा᳘जिष्ठस्त्वं᳘ देवेष्व᳘सि भ्रा᳘जिष्ठो ऽह᳘म् मनुॗष्येषु भूयासमि᳘त्येता᳘नि ह वै भ्रा᳘जांस्येता᳘नि वीॗर्याण्यात्म᳘न्धत्ते य᳘स्यैवं᳘ विदु᳘ष एतान्ग्र᳘हान् गृह्ण᳘न्ति॥

Eggeling
  1. The drinking of these (cups is performed by the sacrificers with the resp. texts), ‘O lustrous Agni, lustrous art thou among the gods: may I be lustrous among men!–Most powerful Indra, most powerful art thou among the gods: may I be the most powerful among men!–Most splendid Sūrya, most splendid art thou among the gods: may I be the most splendid among men!’ And, verily, these same splendours, these same powers he takes unto himself for whomsoever, knowing this, they draw these cups.

१३

मूलम् - Weber

तान्वै पृ᳘ष्ठ्ये षडहे᳘ गृह्णीयात्॥
पूर्वे त्र्यह᳘ आग्नेय᳘मेव᳘ प्रथमे᳘ ऽहन्नैन्द्रं᳘ द्विती᳘ये सौर्यं᳘ तृती᳘य एव᳘मेॗवान्वह᳘म्॥

Eggeling
  1. Let him draw them on the first three days of the Pr̥shṭḥya shaḍaha 8; namely, the Agni cup on

the first day, the Indra cup on the second, the Sūrya cup on the third–thus one day by day.

१४

मूलम् - Weber

ता᳘नु है᳘क उ᳘त्तरे त्र्यहे᳘ गृह्णन्ति॥
त᳘दु त᳘था न᳘ कुर्यात्पू᳘र्व एॗवैनांस्त्र्यहे᳘ गृह्णीयाद्यद्यु᳘त्तरे त्र्यहे᳘ ग्रही᳘ष्यन्त्स्यात्पू᳘र्व एॗवैनांस्त्र्यहे᳘ गृहीत्वाथो᳘त्तरे त्र्यहे᳘ गृह्णीयादेव᳘मेव᳘ यथापूर्वं᳘ विश्वजि᳘ति स᳘र्वपृष्ठ एकाह᳘ एव᳘ गृह्यन्ते॥

Eggeling
  1. Some 9, however, draw them on the last three days; but let him not do so: let him rather draw them on the first three days. But should he intend to draw them on the last three days, let him first draw them on the first three days and let him then draw them on the last three days. In like manner they are drawn (all three) in their proper order, on one and the same day, at the Viśvajit 10 with all the Pr̥shṭḥas.

  1. 402:3 Ati-tishṭḥāvānaḥ, lit. ‘standing forth over (all others,’ see IV, 5, 3, 2). In this, as in the preceding Brāhmaṇa, the prefix ati has to do service repeatedly for etymological and symbolical purposes. ↩︎

  2. 402:2 The Dvādaśāha, or twelve days’ performance, forms the connecting link between the so-called Ahīna sacrifices (consisting of between two and twelve press-days) and the sattras, or sacrificial sessions (of twelve press-days and upwards); since it can be performed as one or the other. As a sattra (which seems to be its usual character) it consists of the Daśarātra, or ten nights’ (or days’) period, preceded and followed by an Atirātra, as the prāyaṇīya (opening) and udayanīya (concluding) days. The Daśarātra, on its part, consists of three tryahas (or tridua), viz. a Pr̥shṭḥya shaḍaha (see note 3), and three Ukthya days, the so-called Chandomas (on which see Haug, Ait. Br. Transl. p. 347). These are followed by an Atyagnishṭoma day, called Avivākya (i.e. on which there should be ’no dispute, or quarrel’). ↩︎

  3. 402:4 I.e. cups of Soma ’to be drawn over and above’ (Weber, Ind. Stud. IX, 235; for a different explanation see Haug, Ait. Br. Transl. p. 490). These three grahas are required at the Pr̥shṭḥya shaḍaha, which forms part of the Dvādaśāha (see note 2), and of sacrificial sessions generally. The shaḍaha, or period of six Soma days, which (though itself consisting of two tryaha, or tridua) may be considered as forming a kind of unit in sattras, or sacrificial sessions, is of two kinds, viz. the Abhiplava shaḍaha and the Pr̥shṭḥya shaḍaha. Both require (for the Hotr̥’s pr̥shṭḥa-stotra at the midday pressing) the use of the Rathantara-sāman on uneven, and that of the Br̥hat-sāman on even days. The chief difference between them is that while the pr̥shṭḥa-stotras of the Abhiplava are performed in the ordinary (Agnishṭoma) way, the Pr̥shṭḥya shaḍaha requires their performance in the proper pr̥shṭḥa form, see p. 339, note 2. Besides, while the Abhiplava shaḍaha consists of four Ukthya days, preceded and followed by one Agnishṭoma day; the first day of the Pr̥shṭḥya shaḍaha is an Agnishṭoma, the fourth a Shoḍaśin, the remaining four days being Ukthyas. There is also a difference between the two in regard to the stomas, or forms of chanting, used; for while the Pr̥shṭḥya requires successively one of the six principal stomas (from the Trivr̥t up to the Trayastriṁśa, as given p. 308, note 2) for each day, the Abhiplava requires the first four stomas (Trivr̥t to Ekaviṁśa) for each day, though in a different order. In this respect, three groups or forms are assumed for the performance of the stotras at the Agnishṭoma and Ukthya, viz. the Jyotishṭoma [a. Bahishpavavamāna in the Trivr̥t; b. Ājyastotras and c. Mādhyandina-pavamāna in the Pañcadaśa; d. the Pr̥shṭḥa-stotras and e. Ārbhava-pavavamāna in the Saptadaśa; and f. the Agnishṭoma sāman in the Ekaviṁśa stoma]; the Goshṭoma [a. Pañcadaśa; b. Trivr̥t; c. Saptadaśa; e. f. (and g. Ukthastotras) Ekaviṁśa]; and Āyushṭoma [a. Trivr̥t; b. Pañcadaśa; c. d. Saptadaśa; e. f. g. Ekaviṁśa]. These forms are distributed over the two tridua of the Abhiplava in the order: Jyotishṭoma, Goshṭoma, Āyushṭoma; Goshṭoma, Āyushṭoma, Gyotishṭoma. ↩︎ ↩︎

  4. 403:1 Lit. even as they are now the superiority, i.e. a superior power. ↩︎

  5. त्वद्यत्वत् AB. ↩︎

  6. 404:1 That is, the right lung (kloman), the left lung being called by a different name. See St. Petersb. Dict. s. v. ↩︎

  7. 405:1 The Kāṇvas use a different formula, viz. Rig-veda IX, 66, 19. See Vāj. S. ed. Weber, p. 254 (XII). ↩︎

  8. 405:2 See page 402, note 4. In conjunction with the Rathantara (Sāma-veda II, 30-31) and Br̥hat (II, 159-60) sāmans, the other four principal pr̥shṭḥa sāmans–viz. the Vairūpa (II, 212-13), Vairāja (II, 277-9), Śākvara (II, 1151-3; or Mahānāmnī, 1-3), and Raivata (II, 434-6)–are used respectively by the Hotr̥ on the last four days of the shaḍaha. As regards the Hotr̥’s assistants, while the Maitrāvaruṇa always uses the same sāman, as at the Agnishṭoma, viz. the Vāmadevya (II, 32-34), the sāmans used by the other Hotrakas are given in the Sāma-veda immediately after the respective sāman of the Hotr̥, mentioned above. ↩︎

  9. 406:1 The Kāṇva text ascribes this practice to the Carakas. ↩︎

  10. 406:2 Regarding the sacrificial session, called Gavām ayana, of which the Viśvajit forms part, see p. 426, note 3. ↩︎
