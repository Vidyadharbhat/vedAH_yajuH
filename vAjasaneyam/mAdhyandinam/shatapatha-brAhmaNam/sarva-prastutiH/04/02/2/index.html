Eggeling
  1. Having then wrapped up (the bowl in) a fringed filtering-cloth, he utters ‘Hi·πÖ!’ Now that same speech (VƒÅc, fem.), being unsupported, lay exhausted. By means of the ‘Hi·πÖ’ the gods infused breath into that exhausted speech, for the ‘Hi·πÖ’ is breath, the ‘Hi·πÖ’ is indeed breath: hence one cannot utter the sound ‘hi·πÖ’ after closing his nostrils. By means of that breath she rose again, for when one who is exhausted takes breath, he rises again. And in like manner does he now infuse breath into the exhausted speech by means of the ‘Hi·πÖ,’ and through. that breath she rises again. Thrice he utters the ‘Hi·πÖ,’ for threefold is the sacrifice.
