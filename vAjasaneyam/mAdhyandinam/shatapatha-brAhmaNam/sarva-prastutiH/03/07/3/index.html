०१

मूलम् - Weber

पशु᳘श्च वै यू᳘पश्च॥
न वा᳘ ऋते यू᳘पात्पशुमा᳘लभन्ते कदा᳘ चन तद्यत्त᳘था न᳘ ह वा᳘ एत᳘स्मा अ᳘ग्रे पश᳘वश्चक्षमिरे यद᳘न्नम᳘भिविष्यन्य᳘थेदम᳘न्नम् भूता य᳘था हैॗवायं᳘ द्विपात्पु᳘रुष उ᳘छ्रित एव᳘ᳫं᳘ हैव᳘ द्विपा᳘द उ᳘छ्रिताश्चेरुः॥

Eggeling
  1. There are both an animal and a sacrificial stake, for never do they immolate an animal without a stake. And as to why this is so:–well, animals did not at first submit thereto that they should become food, as they are now become food; for just as man here walks two-footed and erect, so did they walk two-footed and erect.

०२

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘तो देवा᳘ एतं व᳘ज्रं ददृशुः॥
यद्यू᳘पं तमु᳘छिश्रियुस्तस्माद्भीषा प्रा᳘व्लीयन्त 1 त᳘तश्च᳘तुष्पादा अभवंस्ततो᳘ ऽन्नमभवन्य᳘थेदम᳘न्नम् भूता᳘ एत᳘स्मै हि वा᳘ एते᳘ ऽतिष्ठन्त त᳘स्माद्यू᳘प एव᳘ पशुमा᳘लभन्ते न᳘र्ते यू᳘पात्कदा᳘ चन᳟॥

Eggeling
  1. Then the gods perceived that thunderbolt, to wit, the sacrificial stake; they raised it, and from fear thereof they (the animals) shrunk together and thus became four-footed, and thus became food, as they are now become food, for they submitted thereto: wherefore they immolate the animal only at a stake and never without a stake.

०३

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थोपाकृ᳘त्य पशु᳘म्॥
अग्नि᳘म् मथित्वा नि᳘युनक्ति तद्यत्त᳘था न᳘ ह वा᳘ एत᳘स्मा अ᳘ग्रे पश᳘वश्चक्षमिरे य᳘द्धविर᳘भविष्यन्य᳘थैनानिद᳘ᳫं᳘ हवि᳘र्भूतानग्नौ जु᳘ह्वति ता᳘न्देवा᳘ उपनि᳘रुरुधुस्त᳘ उपनि᳘रुद्धाॗ नोपा᳘वेयुः॥

Eggeling
  1. Having driven up the victim, and churned the fire, he binds it (to the stake). And as to why this is so:–well, animals did not at first submit thereto that they should become sacrificial food, as they are now become sacrificial food and are offered up in the fire. The gods secured them: even thus secured they did not resign themselves.

०४

मूलम् - Weber

ते᳘ होचुः॥
न वा᳘ इॗमे ऽस्य या᳘मं विदुर्य᳘दग्नौ᳘ 2 हविर्जु᳘ह्वतिॗ नैता᳘म् प्रतिष्ठा᳘मुपरु᳘ध्यैव᳘ पशू᳘नग्नि᳘म् मथिॗत्वाग्ना᳘वग्निं᳘ जुहवाम ते᳘ वेदिष्यन्त्येष वै कि᳘ल हवि᳘षो या᳘म एषा᳘ प्रतिॗष्ठाग्नौ वै कि᳘ल हवि᳘र्जुह्वती᳘ति त᳘तो ऽभ्य᳘वैष्यन्ति त᳘तो रात᳘मनस आलम्भा᳘य भविष्यन्ती᳘ति॥

Eggeling
  1. They spake, ‘Verily, these (animals) know not the manner of this, that it is in fire that sacrificial food is offered, nor (do they know) that secure resort (the fire): let us offer fire into the fire after securing the animals and churning the fire, and they will know that this truly is the manner of sacrificial food,

this its resort; that it is truly in fire that sacrificial food is offered: and accordingly they will resign themselves, and will be favourably disposed to the slaughtering.’

०५

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘ उपरु᳘ध्यैव᳘ पशू᳘न्॥
अग्नि᳘म् मथिॗत्वाग्नावग्नि᳘मजुहुवुॗस्ते ऽविदुरेष वै कि᳘ल हवि᳘षो या᳘म एषा᳘ प्रतिॗष्ठाग्नौ वै कि᳘ल ह᳘विर्जुह्वती᳘ति त᳘तो ऽभ्य᳘वायंस्त᳘तो रात᳘मनस आलम्भा᳘याभवन्॥

Eggeling
  1. Having, then, first secured the animals, and churned the fire, they offered fire into the fire; and then they (the animals) knew that this truly is the manner of sacrificial food, this its resort; that it is truly in fire that sacrificial food is offered. And accordingly they resigned themselves, and became favourably disposed to the slaughtering.

०६

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘थो एॗवैष᳘ एतत्॥
उपरु᳘ध्यैव᳘ पशु᳘मग्नि᳘म् मथिॗत्वाग्ना᳘वग्निं᳘ जुहोति स᳘ वेदैष वै कि᳘ल हवि᳘षो या᳘म एषा᳘ प्रतिॗष्ठाग्नौ वै कि᳘ल हवि᳘र्जुह्वती᳘ति त᳘तो ऽभ्य᳘वैति त᳘तो रात᳘मना आलम्भा᳘य भवति त᳘स्मादुपाकृ᳘त्य पशु᳘मग्नि᳘म् मथित्वा नि᳘युनक्ति॥

Eggeling
  1. And in like manner does he now offer fire into the fire, after securing the animal and churning the fire. It (the animal) knows that this truly is the manner of sacrificial food, this its resort; that it is truly in fire that sacrificial food is offered; and accordingly it resigns itself and becomes favourably disposed to the slaughtering. Therefore having driven up the victim and churned the fire, he binds it (to the stake).

०७

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘दाहुः᳟᳟॥
नोपा᳘कुर्याॗन्नाग्नि᳘म् मन्थेद्रशना᳘मेॗवादाया᳘ञ्जसोपपरे᳘त्याभिधा᳘य नि᳘युञ्ज्यादि᳘ति त᳘दु त᳘था न᳘ कुर्याद्यथा᳘धर्मं तिरश्च᳘था चि᳘कीर्षेदेवं तत्त᳘स्मादेत᳘देॗवानुप᳘रीयात्॥

Eggeling
  1. As to this they say, ‘Let him not drive up (the victim), nor churn the fire; but having taken the rope and straightway gone thither and put (the rope) round it, let him bind it.’ Let him, however, not do this; for it would be as if he intended to commit secretly some lawless action. Let him therefore go round there.

०८

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ तृ᳘णमादा᳘योपा᳘करोति॥
द्विती᳘यवान्नि᳘रुणधा इ᳘ति द्विती᳘यवान्हि᳘ वीर्य᳘वान्॥

Eggeling
  1. Then, taking a straw, he drives it up, thinking, ‘having a companion, I will secure it;’ for he who has a companion is strong.

०९

मूलम् - Weber

स तृ᳘णमा᳘दत्ते॥
उपावी᳘रसीत्यु᳘प हि᳘ द्वितीयो᳘ ऽवति त᳘स्मादाहोपावी᳘रसीत्यु᳘प देवान्दै᳘वीर्वि᳘शः प्रागुरि᳘ति दै᳘व्यो वा᳘ एता वि᳘शो य᳘त्पशवो᳘ ऽस्थिषत देवे᳘भ्य इ᳘त्येॗवैत᳘दाह यदाहो᳘प देवान्दै᳘वीर्वि᳘शः प्रा᳘गुरि᳘ति॥

Eggeling
  1. He takes the straw with (Vāj. S. VI, 7), ‘Thou art a cheerer!’ for a companion does cheer one: therefore he says, ‘Thou art a cheerer.’ ‘The celestial hosts have approached the gods;’

the celestial hosts, forsooth, are those beasts: ’they have submitted to the gods’ he means to say, when he says, ‘The celestial hosts have approached the gods.’

१०

मूलम् - Weber

उशि᳘जो व᳘ह्नितमा᳘निति॥
विद्वा᳘ᳫं᳘सो हि᳘ देवास्त᳘स्मादाहोशि᳘जो व᳘ह्नितमानि᳘ति॥

Eggeling
  1. ‘The considerate 3, best of leaders;’ for the gods are wise: therefore he says, ‘The considerate, best of leaders.’

११

मूलम् - Weber

दे᳘व त्वष्टर्व᳘सु रमे᳘ति॥
त्व᳘ष्टा वै᳘ पशूना᳘मीष्टे पश᳘वो व᳘सु ता᳘नेत᳘द्देवा अ᳘तिष्ठमानांस्त्व᳘ष्टारमब्रुवन्नुपनि᳘मदे᳘ति यदा᳘ह देव त्वष्टर्व᳘सु र᳘मेति॥

Eggeling
  1. ‘O divine Tvashṭr̥, settle the wealth!’ for Tvashṭr̥ is lord of beasts (cattle), and wealth means cattle, it is with regard to those which did not submit that the gods then said to Tvashṭr̥, ‘Quiet them,’ when he says, ‘O divine Tvashṭr̥, settle the wealth!’

१२

मूलम् - Weber

हव्या᳘ ते स्वदन्तामि᳘ति॥
यदा वा᳘ एत᳘ एत᳘स्मा अ᳘ध्रियन्त य᳘द्धविर᳘भविष्यंस्त᳘स्मादाह हव्या᳘ ते स्वदन्तामि᳘ति॥

Eggeling
  1. ‘May the offerings be relished by thee!’ Since they themselves submitted thereto that they should become sacrificial food, therefore he says, ‘May the offerings be relished by thee!’

१३

मूलम् - Weber

रे᳘वती र᳘मध्वमि᳘ति॥
रेव᳘न्तो हि᳘ पश᳘वस्त᳘स्मादाह रे᳘वती र᳘मध्वमि᳘ति बृ᳘हस्पते धार᳘या व᳘सूनी᳘ति ब्र᳘ह्म वै बृ᳘हस्पतिः पश᳘वो व᳘सु ता᳘नेत᳘द्देवा अ᳘तिष्ठमानान्ब्र᳘ह्मणैव᳘ पर᳘स्तात्प᳘र्यदधुस्तन्ना᳘त्यायंस्त᳘थो एॗवैनानेष᳘ एतद्ब्र᳘ह्मणैव᳘ पर᳘स्तात्प᳘रिदधाति तन्ना᳘तियन्ति त᳘स्मादाह बृ᳘हस्पते धार᳘या व᳘सूनी᳘ति पा᳘शं कृत्वा प्र᳘तिमुञ्चत्यथा᳘तो नियो᳘जनस्यैव᳟॥

Eggeling
  1. ‘Rejoice, ye prosperous!’ for cattle are prosperous: therefore he says, ‘Rejoice ye prosperous.’ ‘O Lord of prayer, preserve our goods!’ The Lord of prayer, forsooth, is the Brahman; and goods mean cattle: those, which did not submit, the gods on that occasion enclosed with the Brahman on the farther side, and they did not pass over it. And in like manner does he now enclose them with the Brahman on the farther side, and they do not pass over it: therefore he says, ‘O Lord of prayer, preserve our goods!’ Having made a noose he throws it over (the victim) 4. Now then as to the binding itself.

  1. प्राप्लीयन्त Sây. ↩︎

  2. यानं विदु Sây. ↩︎

  3. 180:1 Uśij rather means ‘willing, loving, devoted.’ ↩︎

  4. 180:2 According to the Taitt. authorities (Sāyaṇa on Taitt. S. I, 3, 8), the rope is wound round the right fore-leg and then passed upwards to the head. From Kāty. VI, 3, 27, on the other hand, it would seem that the rope is passed either between the horns (and under the neck?), or round the horns. ↩︎
