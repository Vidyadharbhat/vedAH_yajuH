  1. He then pulls down the tuck 26 (of the sacrificer’s garment) and performs obeisance. The tuck is sacred to the fathers (pitr╠еdevaty─Б): therefore he performs obeisance to them after pulling down the tuck. Now obeisance means worship (or sacrifice): hence he thereby renders them worthy of worship. Six times he performs obeisance; for there are six seasons, and the fathers are the seasons: for this reason he performs obeisance six times. He mutters (V─Бj. S. II, 32 g), ‘Give us houses, O fathers!’ for the fathers are the guardians (─л┼Ыate) of houses; and this is the prayer for blessing at this sacrificial performance. After the cakes have been put back (in the dish containing the remains of boiled rice) he (the sacrificer) smells at (the rice); this (smelling) being the sacrificer’s share. The

(stalks of sacrificial grass) cut with one stroke he puts on the fire; and he also again throws away the firebrand 27.
