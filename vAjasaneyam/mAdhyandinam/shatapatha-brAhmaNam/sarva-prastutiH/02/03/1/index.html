०१

मूलम् - Weber

सू᳘र्यो ह वा᳘ अग्निहोत्रम्॥
तद्य᳘देत᳘स्या अ᳘ग्र आ᳘हुतेरुदैत्त᳘स्मात्सू᳘र्यो ऽग्निहोत्रम्॥

Eggeling
  1. The Agnihotra, doubtless, is the Sun. It is because he rose in front (agre) of that offering 1, that the Agnihotra is the Sun.

०२

मूलम् - Weber

स य᳘त्सायम᳘स्तमिते जुहो᳘ति॥
य᳘ इदं त᳘स्मिन्नि᳘ह सति᳘ जुहवानीत्य᳘थ य᳘त्प्रातर᳘नुदिते जुहो᳘ति य᳘ इदं त᳘स्मिन्निह᳘ सति᳘ जुहवानी᳘ति त᳘स्माद्वै सू᳘र्यो ऽग्निहोत्रमि᳘त्याहुः॥

Eggeling
  1. When he offers in the evening after sunset, he does so thinking, ‘I will offer, while he is here, who is this (offering);’ and when he offers in the morning before sunrise, he does so thinking, ‘I will offer, while he is here, who is this (offering):’ and for this reason, they say, the Agnihotra is the Sun.

०३

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ य᳘दस्तमे᳘ति॥
त᳘दग्ना᳘वेव यो᳘नौ ग᳘र्भो भूत्वा प्र᳘विशति तं ग᳘र्भम् भ᳘वन्तमिमाः स᳘र्वाः प्रजा अ᳘नु ग᳘र्भा भवन्तीलिता 2 हि᳘ शेरे᳘ संजानाना अ᳘थ यद्रा᳘त्रिस्तिर᳘ एवैत᳘त्करोति तिर᳘ इव हि ग᳘र्भाः॥

Eggeling
  1. And when he sets, then he, as an embryo, enters that womb, the fire; and along with him thus becoming an embryo, all these creatures become embryos; for, being coaxed, they lie down contented 3.

The reason, then, why the night envelops that (sun), is that embryos also are, as it were, enveloped.

०४

मूलम् - Weber

स य᳘त्सायम᳘स्तमिते जुहो᳘ति॥
ग᳘र्भमेॗवैतत्स᳘न्तमभिजुहो᳘ति ग᳘र्भᳫं स᳘न्तमभि᳘करोति स यद्ग᳘र्भᳫं स᳘न्तमभिजुहो᳘ति त᳘स्मादिमे ग᳘र्भा अ᳘नश्नन्तो जीवन्ति॥

Eggeling
  1. Now when he offers in the evening after sunset, he offers for the good of that (sun) in the embryo state, he benefits that embryo; and since he offers for the good of that (sun) in the embryo state, therefore embryos here live without taking food.

०५

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ य᳘त्प्रातर᳘नुदिते जुहो᳘ति॥
प्र᳘जनयत्येॗवैनमेतॗत्सो ऽयं ते᳘जो भूत्वा᳘ विभ्राजमान उ᳘देति श᳘श्वद्ध वै नो᳘दियाद्य᳘दस्मिन्नेतामा᳘हुतिं न᳘ जुहुयात्त᳘स्माद्वा एतामा᳘हुतिं जुहोति॥

Eggeling
  1. And when he offers in the morning before sunrise, then he produces that (sun-child) and, having become a light, it rises shining. But, assuredly, it would not rise, were he not to make that offering: this is why he performs that offering.

०६

मूलम् - Weber

स यथा᳘हिस्त्वचो᳘ निर्मुच्ये᳘त॥
एवं रा᳘त्रेः पाप्म᳘ना नि᳘र्मुच्यते य᳘था ह वा अ᳘हिस्त्वचो᳘ निर्मुच्ये᳘तैवᳫं स᳘र्वस्मात्पाप्म᳘नो नि᳘र्मुच्यते य᳘ येवं᳘ विद्वा᳘नग्निहोत्रं᳘ जुहो᳘ति त᳘देत᳘स्यैवा᳘नु प्र᳘जातिमिमाः स᳘र्वाः प्रजा अ᳘नु प्र᳘जायन्ते वि हि᳘ सृज्य᳘न्ते यथार्थम्॥

Eggeling
  1. Even as a snake frees itself from its skin, so does it (the sun-child) free itself from the night, from evil: and, verily, whosoever, knowing this, offers the Agnihotra, he frees himself from all evil, even as a snake frees itself from its skin; and after his birth all these creatures are born; for they are set free according to their inclination.

०७

मूलम् - Weber

स यः᳘ पुॗरादित्य᳘स्यास्तमया᳘त्॥
आहवनी᳘यमुद्ध᳘रत्येते वै वि᳘श्वे देवा᳘ रश्मयो᳘ ऽथ यत्प᳘रम् भाः᳘ प्रजा᳘पतिर्वा स इ᳘न्द्रो वैत᳘दु ह वै वि᳘श्वे देवा᳘ अग्निहोत्रं जु᳘ह्वतो गृहाना᳘गछन्तिस यस्या᳘नुद्धृतमाग᳘छन्ति त᳘स्माद्देवा अपप्र᳘यन्ति तद्वा᳘ अस्मै तॗद्व्यृध्यते य᳘स्माद्देवा᳘ अपप्रय᳘न्ति तस्या᳘नु व्यृद्धिं य᳘श्च वे᳘द य᳘श्च ना᳘नुद्धृतमभ्य᳘स्तमगादि᳘त्याहुः॥

Eggeling
  1. Then, as to his taking out the Āhavanīya (from the Gārhapatya) before the setting of the sun;–the rays, doubtless, are all those gods; and what highest light there is, that, indeed, is either Prajāpati or Indra. Now all the gods approach the house of him who performs the Agnihotra: but whosesoever (offering) they approach before the fire has been taken out, from that the gods turn away, and he fails in it; and after the failure of that (offering) from which the gods turn away, people say, that, whether one knows it or not, the sun went down on account of that (fire) not having been taken out.

०८

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ यः᳘ पुॗरादित्य᳘स्यास्तमया᳘त्॥
आहवनी᳘यमुद्ध᳘रति य᳘था श्रे᳘यस्यागमिष्य᳘त्यावसथेनो᳘पकॢप्तेनोपा᳘सीतैवं तत्स यस्यो᳘द्धृतमाग᳘छन्ति त᳘स्याहवनी᳘यम् प्र᳘विशन्ति त᳘स्याहवनी᳘ये नि᳘विशन्ते॥

Eggeling
  1. And another reason why he takes out the Āhavanīya before the setting of the sun, is this. In like manner as, when one’s better comes to visit

one, he would honour him by trimming his house, so here: for whosesoever (offering) they approach, after the fire has been taken out, his Āhavanīya (house) they enter, in his Āhavanīya they repose.

०९

मूलम् - Weber

स य᳘त्सायम᳘स्तमिते जुहो᳘ति॥
अग्ना᳘वेॗवैभ्य एतत्प्र᳘विष्टेभ्यो जुहोत्य᳘थ य᳘त्प्रातर᳘नुदिते जुहोत्य᳘प्रेतेभ्य एॗवैभ्य एत᳘ज्जुहोति त᳘स्मादुदितहोमि᳘नां वि᳘छिन्नमग्निहोत्र᳘म् मन्यामह इ᳘ति ह स्माहा᳘सुरिर्य᳘था शून्य᳘मावसथ᳘माह᳘रेदेवं तदि᳘ति॥

Eggeling
  1. Now when he offers in the evening after the sun has set, he thereby offers to them after they have entered his fire-house; and when he offers in the morning before sunrise, he offers to them before they go away. Therefore Āsuri said, ‘The Agnihotra of those who offer after sunrise we regard as useless 4: it is as if one were to take food to an empty dwelling.’

१०

मूलम् - Weber

द्वयं वा᳘ इदं जीवनम्॥
मूलि᳘ चैॗवामूलं᳘ च त᳘दुभ᳘यं देवा᳘नाᳫं स᳘न्मनुॗष्या उ᳘पजीवन्ति पश᳘वो मूला ओ᳘षधयो मूलिन्यस्ते᳘ पश᳘वो मूला ओ᳘षधीर्मूलि᳘नीर्जॗग्ध्वापः᳘ पीत्वा त᳘त एष र᳘सः स᳘म्भवति॥

Eggeling
  1. That which affords (the means of) subsistence is of two kinds; namely, either rooted or rootless. On both of these, which belong to the gods, men subsist. Now cattle are rootless and plants are rooted. From the rootless cattle eating the rooted plants and drinking water, that juice is produced.

११

मूलम् - Weber

स य᳘त्सायम᳘स्तमिते जुहो᳘ति॥
अस्य र᳘सस्य जी᳘वनस्य देवे᳘भ्यो जुहवानि य᳘देषामिदᳫं स᳘दुपजी᳘वाम इ᳘ति स यत्त᳘तो रा᳘त्र्याश्ना᳘ति हुतोछिष्ठ᳘मेव त᳘न्निर᳘वत्तबल्यश्नाति हुतोछिष्ट᳘स्यॗ ह्येॗवाग्निहोत्रं जुह्वदशिता॥

Eggeling
  1. Now when he offers in the evening after sunset, he does so thinking, ‘I will offer to the gods of this life-giving juice: we subsist on this which belongs to them.’ And when he afterwards takes his evening meal, he eats what remains of the offering, and whereof oblative portions (bali) have been distributed all round 5; for he who performs the Agnihotra eats only what remains of the offering.

१२

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ य᳘त्प्रातरनुदिते जुहो᳘ति॥
अस्य र᳘सस्य जी᳘वनस्य देवे᳘भ्यो जुहवानि य᳘देषामिदᳫं स᳘दुपजी᳘वाम इ᳘ति स यत्ततो᳘ ऽह्नाश्ना᳘ति हुतोछिष्ट᳘मेव त᳘न्निर᳘वत्तबल्यश्नाति हुतोछिष्ट᳘स्यॗ ह्येॗवाग्निहोत्रं जु᳘ह्वदशिता॥

Eggeling
  1. And when he offers in the morning before sunrise, he does so thinking, ‘I will offer to the gods of this life-giving juice: we subsist on this which belongs to them.’ And when he afterwards takes his meal in the day-time, he eats what remains of the

offering, and whereof oblative portions have been distributed all round; for he who performs the Agnihotra eats only what remains of the offering.

१३

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘दाहुः॥
स᳘मेॗवान्ये᳘ यज्ञास्ति᳘ष्ठन्ते ऽग्निहोत्र᳘मेव न सं᳘तिष्ठते᳘ ऽपि द्वा᳘दशसंवत्सरम᳘न्तवदेवा᳘थैत᳘देॗवानन्त᳘ᳫं᳘ सायᳫं हि᳘ हुत्वा वे᳘द प्रात᳘र्होष्यामी᳘ति प्रात᳘र्हुत्वा᳘ वेद पु᳘नः साय᳘ᳫं᳘ होष्यामी᳘ति त᳘देतद᳘नुपस्थितमग्निहोत्रं तस्या᳘नुपस्थितिमन्व᳘नुपस्थिता इमाः᳘ प्रजाः प्र᳘जायन्ते᳘ ऽनुपस्थितो ह वै᳘ श्रिया᳘ प्रज᳘या प्र᳘जायते य᳘ एव᳘मेतद᳘नुपस्थितमग्निहोत्रं वेद॥

Eggeling
  1. Here now they say,–All other sacrifices come to an end, but the Agnihotra does not come to an end. Although that which lasts for twelve years is indeed limited, this (Agnihotra) is nevertheless unlimited, since, when one has offered in the evening, he knows that he will offer in the morning; and when one has offered in the morning, he knows that he will again offer in the evening. Hence that Agnihotra is unlimited, and in consequence of this its unlimitedness, creatures are here born unlimited. And, verily, he who thus knows the unlimitedness of the Agnihotra, is himself born unlimited in prosperity and offspring.

१४

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘द्दुग्ध्वा᳘धिश्रयति॥
शृतमसदि᳘ति त᳘दाहुर्यर्ह्यु᳘दन्तं त᳘र्हि जुहुयादि᳘ति तद्वै नो᳘दन्तं कुर्यादुप ह दहेद्यदु᳘दन्तं कुर्याद᳘प्रजज्ञि 6 वै रे᳘त उ᳘पदग्धं त᳘स्मान्नो᳘दन्तं कुर्यात्॥

Eggeling
  1. Having milked 7 he puts that (milk) on (the Gārhapatya fire), because it has to be cooked. Here now they say, ‘When it rises to the brim, then we shall offer it!’ He must not however let it rise to the brim, since he would burn it, if he were to let it rise to the brim; and unproductive indeed is burnt seed: he must not, therefore, let it rise to the brim.

१५

मूलम् - Weber

अधिश्रि᳘त्यैव᳘ जुहुयात्॥
यॗन्न्वेॗवैत᳘दग्ने 8 रे᳘तस्ते᳘नॗ न्वेव᳘ शृतं य᳘द्वेनदग्ना᳘वधिश्र᳘यन्ति ते᳘नो एव᳘ शृतं त᳘स्मादधिश्रि᳘त्यैव᳘ जुहुयात्॥

Eggeling
  1. He should not offer it without having put it on the fire; for since this is Agni’s seed, therefore it is hot (śr̥ta, ‘cooked’); and by putting it on the

fire, it is indeed heated: let him, therefore, offer (of the milk) only after he has put it on the fire.

१६

मूलम् - Weber

तद᳘वज्योतयति॥
शृतं᳘ वेदानीत्य᳘थापः᳘ प्रत्या᳘नयति शा᳘न्त्यैॗ न्वेव र᳘सस्यो चैव᳘ सर्वत्वा᳘येदᳫं हि᳘ यदा व᳘र्षत्यथौ᳘षधयो जायन्त ऽओ᳘षधीर्जॗग्ध्वापः᳘ पीत्वा त᳘त एष र᳘सः स᳘म्भवति त᳘स्मादु र᳘सस्यो चैव᳘ सर्वत्वा᳘य त᳘स्माद्य᳘द्येनं क्षीरं के᳘वलम् पा᳘ने ऽभ्याभ᳘वेदुदस्तोकमा᳘श्चोतयितवै᳘ ब्रूयाछा᳘न्त्यैॗ न्वेव र᳘सस्यो चैव᳘ सर्वत्वाय॥

Eggeling
  1. He illumines it (with a burning straw) 9 in order that he may know when it is done. He then pours some water to it (with the sruva), both for the sake of appeasement, and in order to supplement the juice. For when it rains here; then plants spring up; and in consequence of the plants being eaten and the water drunk, this juice is produced: hence it is in order to supplement the juice (that he pours water to it); and therefore, if it should happen to him to have to drink pure milk, let him have one drop of water poured into it, both for the sake of appeasement, and in order to supplement the juice.

१७

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ चतुरु᳘न्नयति॥
चतुर्धाविहित᳘ᳫं᳘ हीदम् पयो᳘ ऽथ समि᳘धमादा᳘योदा᳘द्रवति समिद्धहोमा᳘यैव सो᳘ ऽनुपसाद्य पू᳘र्वामा᳘हुतिं जुहोति स य᳘दुपसाद᳘येद्य᳘था य᳘स्मा अ᳘शनमाहरिश्यन्त्स्यात्त᳘दन्तरा᳘ निदध्या᳘देवं तद᳘थ यद᳘नुपसाद्य य᳘था य᳘स्मा अ᳘शनमाह᳘रेत्त᳘स्मा आहृ᳘त्यैॗवोपनिदध्या᳘देवं त᳘दुपसाद्यो᳘त्तरां ना᳘नावीर्ये एॗवैने एत᳘त्करोति म᳘नश्च ह वै वा᳘क्चैते आ᳘हुति तन्म᳘नश्चैॗवैतद्वा᳘चं च व्या᳘वर्तयति त᳘स्मादिदम् म᳘नश्च वा᳘क्च समान᳘मेव सन्ना᳘नेव॥

Eggeling
  1. Thereupon he ladles four times (milk with the sruva into the Agnihotra ladle 10), for in a fourfold way was that milk supplied 11. He then takes a kindling-stick (samidh), and hastes up (to the Āhavanīya, with the ladle) to make the libation on the burning (stick) 12. He offers the first libation

(pūrvāhuti) without putting down (the spoon) beside (the fire, on the grass-bunch). For, were he to put it down beside (the fire), it would be as if, in taking food to somebody, one were to put it down on one’s way thither. But when (he makes the libation) without previously putting it down, it is as if, in taking food to somebody, one puts it down only after taking it to him. The second (libation he then makes) after putting it down: he thereby makes these two (libations) of various vigour. Now these two: libations are mind and speech: hence he thereby separates mind and speech from each other; and thus mind and speech, even while one and the same (samāna), are still distinct (nānā).

१८

मूलम् - Weber

स वै द्वि᳘रग्नौ᳘ जुहोति॥
द्विरु᳘पमार्ष्टि द्विः प्रा᳘श्नाति चतुरु᳘न्नयति तद्द᳘श द᳘शाक्षरा वै᳘ विरा᳘ड्विराड्वै᳘ यज्ञस्त᳘द्विरा᳘जमेॗवैत᳘द्यज्ञ᳘मभिस᳘म्पादयति॥

Eggeling
  1. Twice he offers in the fire, twice he wipes (the spout of the spoon), twice he eats (of the milk), and four times he ladles 13;–these are ten (acts), for

of ten syllables consists the virāj stanza, and the sacrifice is virāj (shining): he thereby converts the sacrifice into the virāj.

१९

मूलम् - Weber

स य᳘दग्नौ᳘ जुहो᳘ति॥
त᳘द्देवे᳘षु जुहोति त᳘स्माद्देवाः᳘ सन्त्य᳘थ य᳘दुपमा᳘र्ष्टि त᳘त्पितृ᳘षु चौ᳘षधीषु च जुहोति त᳘स्मात्पित᳘रश्चौ᳘षधयश्च सन्त्य᳘थ य᳘द्धुत्वा᳘ प्राश्ना᳘ति त᳘न्मनुॗष्येषु जुहोति त᳘स्मान्मनुॗष्याः सन्ति॥

Eggeling
  1. Now what he offers up in the fire, that he offers to the gods; and thereby the gods are (admitted to the sacrifice) 14. And what he wipes off (the spoons), that he offers to the fathers and plants; and thereby the fathers and plants are (admitted). And what he eats after offering, that he offers to men; and thereby men are (admitted).

२०

मूलम् - Weber

या वै᳘ प्रजा᳘ यज्ञे᳘ ऽनन्वाभक्ताः॥
प᳘राभूता वै ता᳘ एव᳘मेॗवैतद्या᳘ इमाः᳘ प्रजा अ᳘पराभूतास्ता᳘ यज्ञमुख आ᳘भजति ते᳘नो ह पश᳘वो ऽन्वा᳘भक्ता य᳘न्मनुॗष्यान᳘नु पश᳘वः॥

Eggeling
  1. Verily, the creatures that are not allowed to take part in the sacrifice are forlorn; to those creatures that are not forlorn he thus offers a share at the opening of the sacrifice; and thus beasts (cattle) are made to share in it along with (men), since beasts are behind men 15.

२१

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘दु होवाच या᳘ज्ञवल्क्यः॥
न वै᳘ यज्ञ᳘ इव म᳘न्तवै᳘ पाकयज्ञ᳘ इव वा इ᳘तीदᳫं हि य᳘दन्य᳘स्मिन्यज्ञे᳘ स्रुच्य᳘वद्य᳘ति स᳘र्वं त᳘दग्नौ᳘ जुहोत्य᳘थैत᳘दग्नौ᳘ हुॗत्वोत्सृप्या᳘चामति नि᳘र्लेढि त᳘दस्य पाकयज्ञ᳘स्येवे᳘ति त᳘दस्य त᳘त्पशव्यं᳘ रूपम् पशॗव्यो हि पाकयज्ञः॥

Eggeling
  1. On this point Yājñavalkya said, ‘It (the Agnihotra) must not be looked upon as a (havis-) sacrifice, but as a domestic sacrifice (pākayajña); for while in any other (havis-)sacrifice he pours into the fire all that he cuts off (from the sacrificial dish and puts) into the offering spoon,–here, after offering and stepping outside 16, he sips water and licks out (the milk); and this indeed (is a characteristic) of the domestic offering.’ This then is the animal

characteristic 17 of that (Agnihotra), for the domestic offering pertains to beasts (or cattle).

२२

मूलम् - Weber

सैषै᳘काहुति᳘रेवा᳘ग्रे॥
या᳘मेवामू᳘म् प्रजा᳘पतिर᳘जुहोद᳘थ य᳘देत᳘ एत᳘त्पॗश्चेवा᳘ध्रियन्ताग्निॗर्यो ऽयम् प᳘वते सू᳘र्यस्त᳘स्मादेषा᳘ द्वितीया᳘हुतिर्हूयते॥

Eggeling
  1. Now the first of these libations, doubtless, is the same as that which Prajāpati offered in the beginning 18; and as those (gods) thereupon continued (to sacrifice) 19,–namely, Agni, that blower (Vāyu), and Sūrya,–so this second libation is offered.

२३

मूलम् - Weber

सा या पूर्वा᳘हुतिः᳟᳟॥
साग्निहोत्र᳘स्य देव᳘ता त᳘स्मात्त᳘स्यै जुहोत्य᳘थ यो᳘त्तरा स्विष्टकृद्भाजन᳘मेव सा त᳘स्मात्ता᳘मुत्तरार्धे जुहोत्येषा हि दि᳘क् स्विष्टकृ᳘तस्त᳘न्मिथुना᳘यैॗवैषा᳘ द्वितीया᳘हुतिर्हूयते द्वन्द्वᳫं हि᳘ मिथुन᳘म् प्रज᳘ननम्॥

Eggeling
  1. What first libation (pūrvāhuti) is made, that is the deity of the Agnihotra 20, and to that (deity) it is accordingly offered; and what second one (uttarāhuti) is made, that indeed is equivalent to the Svishṭakr̥t (Agni, the maker of good offering); whence he offers it on the north part (of the fire), since that is the region of the Svishṭakr̥t 21. Moreover, this second libation is made in order to effect a pairing, for a couple forms a productive pair.

२४

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘द्द्वय᳘मेॗवैते आ᳘हुती॥
भूतं᳘ चैव᳘ भविष्य᳘च्च जातं᳘ च जनिष्य᳘माणं चा᳘गतं चाशा चाद्य᳘ च श्व᳘श्च त᳘द्द्वय᳘मेवानु 22 ॥

Eggeling
  1. These two libations, then, form a duad: the past and the future, the born and the to-be-born, the actual 23 and the hope, the to-day and the morrow,–(these are) after the manner of that duad.

२५

मूलम् - Weber

आॗत्मैव᳘ भूत᳘म्॥
अद्धा हि तद्य᳘द्भू᳘त᳘मॗद्धो तद्य᳘दात्मा᳘ प्रॗजैव᳘ भविष्यद᳘नद्धा हि तद्य᳘द्भविष्यद᳘नद्धो तद्य᳘त्प्रजा॥

Eggeling
  1. The past is the self, for certain is that which is past, and certain also is that which is a self. The future, on the other hand, is progeny; for uncertain is that which is to be, and uncertain also is progeny.

२६

मूलम् - Weber

आॗत्मैव जात᳘म्॥
अद्धा हि तद्य᳘ज्जात᳘मॗद्धो तद्य᳘दात्मा᳘ प्रॗजैव᳘ जनिष्य᳘माणम᳘नद्धा हि तद्य᳘ज्जनिष्य᳘माणम᳘नद्धो तद्य᳘त्प्रजा॥

Eggeling
  1. The born is the self, for certain is that which is born, and certain also is the self. The to-be-born,

on the other hand, is progeny; for uncertain is what is to be born, and uncertain also is progeny.

२७

मूलम् - Weber

आ᳘त्मैवा᳘गतम्॥
अद्धा हि तद्यदा᳘गतमॗद्धो तद्य᳘दात्मा᳘ प्रॗजैॗवाशा᳘नद्धा हि तद्य᳘दाशा᳘नद्धो तद्य᳘त्प्रजा॥

Eggeling
  1. The actual is the self, for certain is what is actual, and certain also is the self. And hope is progeny, for uncertain is hope, and uncertain also is progeny.

२८

मूलम् - Weber

आॗत्मैॗवाद्य॥
अद्धा हि तद्य᳘दॗद्याद्धो तद्य᳘दात्मा᳘ प्रॗजैव श्वो᳘ ऽनद्धा हि तद्यछ्वो᳘ ऽनद्धो तद्य᳘त्प्रजा॥

Eggeling
  1. The to-day is the self, for certain is what is to-day, and certain also is the self. The morrow is progeny, for uncertain is the morrow, and uncertain also is progeny.

२९

मूलम् - Weber

सा या पूर्वा᳘हुतिः॥
सात्मा᳘नमभि᳘ हूयते ताम् म᳘न्त्रेण जुहोत्यद्धा हि तद्यन्म᳘न्त्रोॗ ऽद्धो तद्य᳘दात्मा᳘ ऽथ यो᳘त्तरा सा᳘ प्रजा᳘मभि᳘ हूयते तां᳘ तूष्णीं᳘ जुहोत्य᳘नद्धा हि तद्य᳘त्तूष्णीमनद्धो तद्य᳘त्प्रजा॥

Eggeling
  1. Now that first libation is offered on account of the self: he offers it with a sacred text, for certain is the sacred text, and certain also is the self. And that second one is offered on account of progeny: he offers it silently, for uncertain is what (is done) silently, and uncertain also is progeny.

३०

मूलम् - Weber

स᳘ जुहोति॥
अग्निर्ज्यो᳘तिर्ज्यो᳘तिरग्निः स्वाहेत्य᳘थ प्रातः सू᳘र्यो ज्यो᳘तिर्ज्योतिः सू᳘र्यः स्वाहे᳘ति त᳘त्सत्ये᳘नैव᳘ हूयते यदा ह्येवसू᳘र्यो ऽस्तमेत्य᳘थाग्निर्ज्यो᳘तिर्यदा सू᳘र्य उदेत्य᳘थ सू᳘र्यो ज्यो᳘तिर्यद्वै᳘ सत्ये᳘न हूय᳘ते त᳘द्देवा᳘न् गछति॥

Eggeling
  1. [In the evening] he offers (the first libation), with the text (Vāj. S. III, 9, 10), ‘Agni is the light, the light is Agni, Svāhā!’ and in the morning with, ‘Sūrya (the sun) is the light, the light is Sūrya, Svāhā!’ Thus offering is made with the truth; for, truly, when the sun goes down, then Agni (fire) is the light, and when the sun rises, then Sūrya is the light; and whatever is offered with the truth, that, indeed, goes to the gods.

३१

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘दु हैत᳘देवा᳘रुणये ब्रह्मवर्चस᳘कामाय तक्षा᳘नूवा᳘चाग्निर्व᳘र्चो ज्यो᳘तिर्व᳘र्चः सू᳘र्यो व᳘र्चो ज्यो᳘तिर्व᳘र्च इ᳘ति ब्रह्मवर्चसी᳘ हैव᳘ भवति य᳘ एवं᳘ विद्वा᳘नग्नि होत्रं᳘ जुहोति॥

Eggeling
  1. Here now Takshan recited for Āruṇi 24, who wished to obtain holy lustre (brahmavarcasa, inspired nature), ‘Agni is lustre, light is lustre;’–‘Sūrya is lustre, light is lustre.’ Holy lustre, therefore, he obtains whosoever, knowing this, thus offers the Agnihotra.

३२

मूलम् - Weber

तद्व᳘स्त्येव᳘ प्रज᳘ननस्येव रूप᳘म्॥
अग्निर्ज्यो᳘तिर्ज्यो᳘तिरग्निः स्वा᳘हेति त᳘दुभय᳘तो ज्यो᳘ती रे᳘तो देवत᳘या प᳘रिगृह्णात्युभयतः परिगृहीतं वै रे᳘तः प्र᳘जायते त᳘दुभय᳘त एॗवैत᳘त्परिगृ᳘ह्य प्र᳘जनयति॥

Eggeling
  1. That (other text), however, has the characteristic form of generation. In saying, ‘Agni is the light, the light is Agni, Svāhā!’ he encloses that seed, the light, on both sides with the deity; and the seed, thus enclosed on both sides, is brought forth: thus enclosing it on both sides he causes it to be brought forth.

३३

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ प्रातः॥
सू᳘र्यो ज्यो᳘तिर्ज्यो᳘तिः सू᳘र्यः स्वाहे᳘ति त᳘दुभय᳘तो ज्यो᳘ती रे᳘तो देवत᳘या प᳘रिगृह्णात्युभयतःपरिगृहीतं वै रे᳘तः प्र᳘जायते त᳘दुभय᳘त एॗवैत᳘त्परिगृ᳘ह्य प्र᳘जनयति त᳘त्प्रज᳘ननस्य रूपम्॥

Eggeling
  1. And when, in the morning, he says, ‘Sūrya is the light, the light is Sūrya, Svāhā!’ he encloses that seed, the light, on both sides with the deity, and the seed, thus enclosed on both sides, is brought forth: thus enclosing it on both sides he causes it to be brought forth; and this, indeed, is the characteristic form of generation.

३४

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘दु होवाच जीवलश्चै᳘लकिः॥
ग᳘र्भमेवा᳘रुणिः करो᳘ति न प्र᳘जनयती᳘ति स᳘ एते᳘नैव᳘ सायं जुहुयात्॥

Eggeling
  1. But Jīvala Cailaki said 25, ‘Āruṇi merely causes conception to take place, not birth: let him therefore offer with that (text, in par. 32) in the evening.

३५

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ प्रातः॥
ज्यो᳘तिः सूर्यः सू᳘र्यो ज्यो᳘तिः स्वाहे᳘ति त᳘द्बहिर्धा ज्यो᳘ती रे᳘तो देवत᳘या करो᳘ति बहिर्धा वै रे᳘तः प्र᳘जातम् भवति त᳘देनत्प्र᳘जनयति॥

Eggeling
  1. Then, in the morning, by the text, “The light is Sūrya, Sūrya is the light,” he places that seed, the light, outside by means of the deity; and the seed thus brought outside he causes to be born.’

३६

मूलम् - Weber

त᳘दाहुः॥
अग्ना᳘वेॗवैत᳘त्सायᳫं सू᳘र्यं जुहोति सू᳘र्ये प्रात᳘रग्निमि᳘ति तद्वै त᳘दुदितहोमि᳘नामेव᳘ यदाॗ ह्येव सू᳘र्यो ऽस्तमेत्य᳘थाग्निर्ज्यो᳘तिर्यदा सू᳘र्य उदेत्य᳘थ सू᳘र्यो ज्यो᳘तिॗर्नास्य सा᳘ परिचॗक्षेय᳘मेव᳘ परिचक्षा यत्त᳘स्यैॗ नाद्धा᳘ देव᳘तायै हूय᳘तेॗ याग्निहोत्र᳘स्य देव᳘ताग्निर्ज्यो᳘तिर्ज्यो᳘तिरग्निः स्वाहे᳘ति त᳘त्र नाग्न᳘ये स्वाहेत्य᳘थ प्रातः सू᳘र्यो ज्यो᳘तिज्यो᳘तिः सू᳘र्यः स्वाहे᳘ति त᳘त्र न सू᳘र्याय स्वाहे᳘ति॥

Eggeling
  1. They also say, ‘In the evening he offers Sūrya in Agni, and in the morning he offers Agni in Sūrya 26.’ Such, indeed, is the case with those who offer after sunrise; for when the sun sets then Agni is the light, and when the sun rises then Sūrya is the light. Here no offence is committed on his (the sacrificer’s) part; but an offence is indeed committed where offering is not made distinctly to that deity (viz. Agni or Sūrya resp.), which is the deity of the Agnihotra.

He says 27, ‘Agni is the light, the light is Agni, Svāhā!’ and not, ‘To Agni Svāhā!’ and in the morning, ‘Sūrya is the light, the light is Sūrya, Svāhā!’ and not, ‘To Sūrya Svāhā!’

३७

मूलम् - Weber

अने᳘नैव᳘ जुहुयात्॥
सजू᳘र्देवे᳘न सवित्रे᳘ति त᳘त्सवितृम᳘त्प्रसवा᳘य सजू रात्र्ये᳘न्द्रवत्ये᳘ति तद्रा᳘त्र्या मिथुनं᳘ करोति से᳘न्द्रं करोती᳘न्द्रो हि᳘ यज्ञ᳘स्य देव᳘ता जुषाणो᳘ अग्नि᳘र्वेतु स्वाहेति त᳘दग्न᳘ये प्रत्य᳘क्षं जुहोति॥

Eggeling
  1. He may also offer (in the evening) with this text (Vāj. S. III, 10), ‘Along with the divine Savitr̥–,’ whereby it (the sacrifice) becomes possessed of Savitr̥ for his impulsion; ‘–along with the Night, wedded to Indra–,’ whereby he effects a union with the night, and makes it (the sacrifice) possessed of Indra, for Indra is the deity of the sacrifice; ‘–may Agni graciously accept! Svāhā!’ whereby he offers to Agni in a direct manner.

३८

मूलम् - Weber

अ᳘थ प्रातः᳟॥
सजू᳘र्देवे᳘न सवित्रे᳘ति त᳘त्सवितृम᳘त्प्रसवा᳘य सजू᳘रुषसे᳘न्द्रवत्येत्यह्ने᳘ति वा तद᳘ह्नां वोष᳘सां वा मिथुनं᳘ करोति से᳘न्द्र करोती᳘न्द्रो हि᳘ यज्ञ᳘स्य देव᳘ता जुषाणः सू᳘र्यो वेतु स्वाहे᳘ति तत्सू᳘र्याय प्रत्य᳘क्षं जुहोति त᳘स्मादेव᳘मेव᳘ जुहुयात्॥

Eggeling
  1. And in the morning with, ‘Along with the divine Savitr̥–,’ whereby it becomes possessed of Savitr̥ for his impulsion; ‘–along with the Dawn, wedded to Indra,’ or ‘along with the Day . . . .–,’ whereby he effects a union either with the day or the dawn 28, and makes it (the sacrifice) possessed of Indra; for Indra is the deity of sacrifice; ‘–may Sūrya graciously accept! Svāhā!’ whereby he offers to Sūrya directly: hence he may offer in this way.

३९

मूलम् - Weber

ते᳘ होचुः॥
को᳘ न इद᳘ᳫं᳘ होष्यती᳘ति ब्राह्मण᳘ एवे᳘ति ब्रा᳘ह्मणेदं᳘ नो जुहुधी᳘ति कि᳘म् मे ततो भविष्यती᳘त्यग्निहोत्रोछिष्ट᳘मेवे᳘ति स य᳘त्स्रुचि परिशिन᳘ष्टि त᳘दग्निहोत्रोछिष्टम᳘थ य᳘त्स्थाल्यां य᳘था परीण᳘हो निर्व᳘पेदेवं तत्त᳘स्मात्तद्य᳘ एव क᳘श्च पिबेत्तद्वै ना᳘ब्राह्मणः पिबेदग्नौ ह्य᳘धिश्र᳘यन्ति त᳘स्मान्ना᳘ब्राह्मणः पिबेत्॥

Eggeling
  1. They then spake, ‘Who shall offer this unto us?’–‘The priest (brāhmaṇa)!’–‘Priest, offer this unto us! What is to be my share then? The residue of the Agnihotra!’ Now what he leaves in

the ladle, that is the residue of the Agnihotra; and what remains in the pot, is as (the rice for oblations which) one takes out from the enclosed part (of the cart) 29. And if any one is to drink it, at least none but a Brāhman must drink it 30: for it is put on the fire (and thereby consecrated), and hence none but a Brāhman must drink it.

Thereupon he lays down the ladle on the kūrca [a grass-bunch, placed behind the Āhavanīya fire-place, to serve as a seat, and to wipe the hands on; according to others, a flat piece of Varaṇa wood], then takes it up again and silently makes the second libation (uttarāhuti) on the north part of the fire. Kāty. IV, 14, 12-17 with Schol. ↩︎
